Abstract

3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) is a promising techniquefor 3D reconstruction, offering efficient training and ren-dering speeds, making it suitable for real-time applications.However, current methods require highly controlled envi-ronments—no moving people or wind-blown elements, andconsistent lighting—to meet the inter-view consistency as-sumption of 3DGS. This makes reconstruction of real-worldcaptures problematic. We present SpotLessSplats, a novelapproach that leverages Stable Diffusion features and ro-bust optimization to effectively ignore transient distractors.Our method achieves state-of-the-art reconstruction qualityboth visually and quantitatively, on casual captures.
